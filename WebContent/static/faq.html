Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is this site?
This is a file-sharing site, accessible via a web browser as well as a WebDAV client.
What is the purpose of this site?
This site enables Marakana instructors and students to share files with each other.
Generally, instructors publish class materials to students while students have the option of sharing their work in order to solicit feedback.
What is the correct URL to access this site?
The base URL for a class with a specific <id> must be in the following format:
https://class-<id>.marakana.com/class/<id>/files/
Note that the URL given to you was most likely shortened - e.g. http://mrkn.co/s0tur1.
Who gets access to this site?
This site is accessible to the registered students and the instructor teaching the class.
The class must be confirmed for this site to be active.
The site is also accessible to Marakana staff - for maintenance reasons.
How do I register for this class, so that I can access this site?
For public classes, you can register via our public schedule page.
For private (on-site) classes, you can register using a special URL/code that is generally provided to you by our training coordinator, your training manager, or the instructor.
Note that your employer may also require that you register through your organization's own learning management system - separate from ours.
How do I log in to this site?
To access this site, you need to log in with the same credentials you use to access Marakana.com site. Your username is your email address.
The login dialog will vary based on the browser/client you are using, but on the protocol level, you'll be using Basic access authentication.
Our use of HTTPS (SSL/TLS) protects the confidentiality of your credentials as they are transmitted over the network.
I forgot my password. What do I do?
You can reset your password via our site. You will need access to your email for this to work.
How do I log out of this site?
With the Basic access authentication, the client typically caches your username/password and sends them on every request. This means that it is up to the client to "forget" your credentials in order to log you out.
  • For browsers, this often means that you have to either close the browser or use some other mechanism (that is browser-specific) to clear current authenticated sessions.
  • For WebDAV clients, you can "log out" by unmounting/ejecting the shared volume. How this is done is client-specific.
I have access to multiple classes. Why do I have to log in to each one separately?
For security reasons, each class is accessible under a unique domain name. Your credentials are domain-specific as far as your browser/client is concerned.
How long will I have access to this site?
You will retain access to this site for as long as you maintain an active account on Marakana.com.
How do I mount this site using WebDAV on MacOS X?
  1. Go to "Finder" application
  2. Go to Menu-bar → "Go" → "Connect to Server..." (or press ⌘K)
  3. Under "Server Address" enter the base URL to this site (i.e. "https://class-<id>.marakana.com/class/<id>/files/")
  4. Click on "Connect"
  5. When presented with a login dialog
    1. Specify "Connect as:" → "Registered User"
    2. Enter your email as the "Name"
    3. Enter your password as the "Password"
    4. Optionally check "Remember this password in my keychain"
    5. Click on "Connect"
  6. Go to "Finder" → "SHARED" (sidebar) → "class-<id>.marakana.com" → "files" to access your files
  7. This share is also accessible via /Volumes/files/ folder (e.g. from "Terminal")

Replace <id> with the actual ID for the class you are trying to access.
How do I mount this site using WebDAV on Ubuntu Linux?
  1. Go to "Places" toolbar
  2. Go to "Connect to Server..."
  3. When presented with the "Connect to Server" dialog
    1. Choose "Secure WebDAV (HTTPS)" as the "Service type"
    2. Enter "class-<id>.marakana.com" as the "Server"
    3. Enter "443" as the "Port"
    4. Enter "/class/<id>/files/" as the "Folder"
    5. Enter your email address as the "User Name"
    6. Optionally "Add bookmark" and specify "Bookmark name" of your choosing
    7. Click on "Connect"
  4. When presented with the "Enter password for /class/<id>" dialog
    1. Enter your password for "Password"
    2. Select "Remember password until you logout" option
    3. Click on "Connect"
  5. Go to "Places" (or "Desktop") → "WebDAV on class-<id>.marakana.com" to access your files
  6. This share is also accessible via "~/.gvfs/WebDAV on class-<id>.marakana.com" folder (e.g. from "Terminal")

Replace <id> with the actual ID for the class you are trying to access.
How do I mount this site using WebDAV on Windows XP?
  1. Make sure that your Windows XP installation has all of the latest Windows Update patches installed ("Start" → "Settings" → "Control Panel" → "Windows Updates")
  2. Go to "Windows Explorer"
  3. Right-click on "My Computer" (sidebar) and select "Map network drive..."
  4. When presented with the "Map Network Drive" dialog, click on "Sign up for online storage or connect to a network server"
  5. Click on "Next >"
  6. Select "Choose another network location..."
  7. Click on "Next >"
  8. Under "Internet or network address" enter the base URL to this site (i.e. "https://class-<id>.marakana.com/class/<id>/files/")
  9. Click on "Next >"
  10. When presented with the "Enter Network Password" dialog
    1. Enter your email address for "User name"
    2. Enter your password for "Password"
    3. Check "Save this password in your password list" option
    4. Click on "OK"
  11. When presented with the "What you want to name this place?" dialog, leave the default name, and click on "Next >"
  12. When presented with the "Completing the Add Network Place Wizard" dialog, leave "Open this network place when I click Finish" checked, and click on "Finish"
  13. Go to "Windows Explorer" → "My Network Places" (sidebar) → "files on class-<id>.marakana.com" to access the shared files

Replace <id> with the actual ID for the class you are trying to access.
How do I mount this site using WebDAV on Windows 7?
  1. Make sure that your Windows 7 installation has all of the latest Windows Update patches installed ("Start" → "All Programs" → "Windows Update")
  2. Go to "Windows Explorer"
  3. Right-click on "Computer" (sidebar) and select "Map network drive..."
  4. When presented with the "Map Network Drive" dialog
    1. Choose any letter you want (e.g. "Z:") for the "Drive" option
    2. enter the base URL to this site (i.e. "https://class-<id>.marakana.com/class/<id>/files/") under "Folder"
    3. Optionally check "Reconnect at logon"
    4. Check "Connect using different credentials"
    5. Click on "Finish"
  5. When presented with the "Windows Security" dialog
    1. Enter your email address for "User name"
    2. Enter your password for "Password"
    3. Optionally check "Remember my credentials" option
    4. Click on "OK"
  6. Go to "Windows Explorer" → "Computer" (sidebar) → "files (\\class-<id>.marakana.com...)" to access the shared files
  7. You can also access your files via the "Command Prompt" if you go to the drive letter assigned during the mapping step (e.g. "Z:").

Replace <id> with the actual ID for the class you are trying to access.

What is the "Download [this directory] as a ZIP file" feature?
This feature allows your web-browser to download any file/directory on this site as a ZIP file.
The archival/compression process happens on-the-fly, at the time of your request, and the generated archives are streamed to your browser and never cached.
What makes README.html files so special?
When accessing https://class-<id>.marakana.com/class/<id>/files/…/foo/ in a web-browser, the contents of …/foo/README.html are automatically embedded into the resulting directory listing.
Note that README.html files should not have the HTML preamble (i.e. only include what would go inside <body> tag).
What kind of content can I upload this site?
We ask that you only upload content related to the training class you are participating in.
We specifically ask that you do not upload content that is illegal, racist, sexist, violent, drug-related, political, religious, inappropriate, promotional, discriminating, copyrighted, confidential (except for class materials), and/or irrelevant.
Will my files be safe? Can I use this for backup?
We provide no guarantees for safe-keeping or ensuring confidentiality of your files and we take no responsibility for leaked, lost or damaged content.
Please save/backup your files in your own durable storage medium. Since this site is meant for (limited) file sharing, please do not upload any sensitive or confidential (except for class materials) content.
